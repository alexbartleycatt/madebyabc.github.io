Hello! I'm Alex, a designer shaping the future of UX and product design.

Based in Aotearoa, I blend design thinking, psychology, and emerging technology to transform ambitious ideas into user-friendly products and digital services.

With over a decade of experience, my mission is to ensure technology serves people, not the other way around.

Whether you're a budding visionary or established titan in your industry, you understand the power of thoughtful design. Your challenges revolve around transforming organisational goals and technical opportunity into user-friendly experiences.

Maybe you seek a collaborator who can grasp the technicalities and translate them into designs that speak to the hearts and minds of people?

I’ve been fortunate to work with a range of intelligent and creative people on all sorts of projects. Together, we’ve had great success applying user centred design and emerging technology to challenges faced by both public and private organisations.

Some highlights include:

Intelligent Avatars for Integral Diagnostics

We helped one of Australasia's largest radiology providers deploy an intelligent avatar across multiple brands under the IDX umbrella. The avatar is designed to make booking an appointment effortless for patients, while being integrated directly with medical and systems to create efficiency and save staff time.

Natural Language Search for NZ Police

In this project we designed and developed a natural language search engine and interface for an internal knowledge base. The key user of this solution is NZ Police knowledge workers who need instant and up-to-date information to perform their jobs effectively. Our solution made a huge amount of technical information accessible with a quick search in the user's own words.

Referral Automation for Mercy Radiology

We helped Mercy Radiology develop a referral automation solution. Using a mix of OCR, AI-based layout detection, and process automation, this solution extracts key fields from dozens of varied referral documents and identifies potentially incorrect or incomplete information. This allowed staff to spend their time on more important work, rather than reading and entering countless referrals each day.

Tech Education for SeniorNet NZ

SeniorNet is an amazing organisation that exists to help anyone over the age of 65 make better use of digital technology. I was lucky enough to guide the creation of two courses focussed on; using Google Search to find information, and using Google Drive for personal creativity and admin. We did this to give senior learners the tools to develop their skills and confidence outside of the classroom.
